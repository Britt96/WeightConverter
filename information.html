Weight Conversion

Definition

The pound (lb), also called the imperial pound, avoirdupois pound, or international pound, is exactly 0.45359237 kilograms. It was popular in the 13th century but has since grown obsolete in most of the world but the US, where it is still used to measure body weight.

The kilogram (kg) was initially defined as equivalent to a liter of water at its freezing point before it was redefined more precisely with the Planck constant. The kilogram is the SI base unit for mass, meaning all other metric mass (weight) units are derived from it. It is equal to 2.20462262 pounds. Unlike the pound, the kilogram is widely used in science, medicine, etc. around the world today except the US.

Formula

The formula used to convert pounds to kilograms is lbs * 0.45359237. Kilograms is converted to pounds by dividing the former by 0.45359237; this app uses both.

Purpose

Conversion from lbs and kgs is used in pediatric nursing, where some medication dosages (mg/kg) are based on the child's weight in kilograms.
